Young Stellar Population of Monoceros Star Cluster (Jun 2022 - PRESENT)
During the Summer of 2022, I worked with Dr. Lynne A. Hillenbrand at Caltech under the Caltech WAVE program, studying the young stellar population of the Mon R2 Star Cluster. I studied the membership of the cluster stars and found new candidate stars using data from the new Gaia Data Release 3. We used the embedded cluster to study processes occurring in young stellar objects by looking at the optical variability of the stars using ZTF. I will be presenting a more updated poster in Jan 2023 at the AAS 241st meeting in Seattle, WA.
Project: RNAAS article, Poster, Presentation, and Abstract

Magnetic Fields in Orion (Aug 2021 - PRESENT)
Starting in August 2021, I started working with Dr. Hector Arce, at Yale, to study magnetic fields in the Orion A cloud. I am funded by the competitive STARS II Academic Fellowship and working on the project as my Senior Thesis. I am studying the alignment of the magnetic field in combination with gas structure maps from the CARMA-NRO Survey. In addition, I have been observing with the WIRC+Pol instrument at Palomar to get magnetic field measurements in more diffuse regions of Orion A.
I will be presenting a more updated poster in April 2023 at the Protostars and Planets VII meeting in Kyoto, Japan.
Project: Quick Presentation, Poster, Manuscript in Progress

Tracers of the Galactic Magnetic Field (Jun 2021 - Dec 2021)
During the Summer of 2021, I worked at Stanford University (Remotely) under the Leadership Alliance Summer Research Early Identification Program (SR-EIP) with Dr. Susan E. Clark. I studied the structure of the nearby Interstellar Medium and traced the Galactic magnetic field using starlight polarization and atomic hydrogen (HI) emission. I studied when these two tracers were aligned or misaligned as functions of the location around the Galaxy (latitude and distance) and through line-of-sight dust polarization. I presented my work at Virtual Leadership Alliance National Symposium (VLAN) and Stanford Humanities and Science Symposium.
Comparison of HI Structures Orientation with Magnetic Field orientation from Planck Dust Emission; Source: Clark, et al, 2015
Project: Poster, Presentation, Abstract
